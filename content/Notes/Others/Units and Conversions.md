# SI Units
SI units share a base10 system, which allows you to make sub-units to represent bigger or smaller values that would be a hassle to write in the base unit:

Example: milligrams are better for smaller vales.

$$0.001\text{g}=1\text{mg}$$

Example: kilograms are better for larger values.

$$1000\text{g}=1\text{kg}$$

# Conversion Factors
Simply multiply these factors to your given to get the desired converted value.

*Note I:  Conversion factors will only cover milli-units to kilo-units; the same rules apply for bigger and smaller units.*

*Note II: to do the reversed conversion, use a reciprocal of the conversion factor*
## Mass
- Grams to kilograms: $$\left(\frac{1\text{kg}}{1000\text{g}}\right)$$
- Grams to hectograms: $$\left(\frac{1\text{hg}}{100\text{g}}\right)$$
- Grams to dekagrams: $$\left(\frac{1\text{dag}}{10\text{g}}\right)$$
- Grams to decigrams: $$\left(\frac{10\text{dg}}{1\text{g}}\right)$$
- Grams to centigrams: $$\left(\frac{100\text{cg}}{1\text{g}}\right)$$
- Grams to milligrams: $$\left(\frac{1000\text{mg}}{1\text{g}}\right)$$
- Grams to grains: $$\left(\frac{15.432\text{gr}}{1\text{g}}\right)$$
- Grams to scruples: $$\left(\frac{1\text{℈}}{1.3\text{g}}\right)$$
- Grams to drams: $$\left(\frac{1\text{ʒ}}{3.89\text{g}}\right)$$
- Grams to ounces ( apothecary ): $$\left(\frac{1\text{℥}}{31.1\text{g}}\right)$$
- Grams to pounds ( apothecary ): $$\left(\frac{1\text{℔}}{373\text{g}}\right)$$
- Grams to ounces ( avoirdupois ): $$\left(\frac{1\text{oz}}{28.35\text{g}}\right)$$
- Grams to pounds ( avoirdupois ): $$\left(\frac{1\text{lb}}{454\text{g}}\right)$$

## Temperature
- Degrees fahrenheit to degrees celsius: $$\degree\text{C}=(\degree\text{F}-32)\times\frac{5}{9}$$
- Degrees celsius to degrees fahrenheit: $$\degree\text{F}=\left(\degree\text{C}\times\frac{9}{5}\right)+32$$
- Degrees celsius to kelvin: $$K=\degree C+273.15$$
